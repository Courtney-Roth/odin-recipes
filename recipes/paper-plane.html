Paper Plane Recipe

Description:

The ideal aperitivo: simple to make, easy to drink.

The drink, invented by the New York bartender Sam Ross, who created the classic modern cocktail the penicillin, has slowly been gaining steam since it was introduced in 2007, showing up on cocktail menus in numerous time zones. It is a rich, immediately likable whiskey sour lent plenty of culinary complexity by the amaro and the Aperol. —Robert Simonson

Ingredients:

  • 1½ ounces amaro (preferably Nonino)
  • 1½ ounces Aperol
  • 1½ ounces bourbon
  • 1½ ounces fresh lemon juice, strained

Steps:

  1. Combine amaro, Aperol, bourbon, and lemon juice in a cocktail shaker.
  2. Fill with ice and shake vigorously until outside of shaker is frosty, about 20 seconds.
  3. Strain into 2 coupe glasses.
